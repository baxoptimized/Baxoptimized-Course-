## ✅ What makes a website "good"

Not "impressive." Just these:
- ✅ Loads fast (under 3 sec, ideally under 1.5)
- ✅ Looks right on every device
- ✅ Clear purpose per page
- ✅ Tells the visitor what to do next
- ✅ Builds trust quickly
- ✅ Easy to update later
- ✅ Doesn't break

> 🔗 This is the same list from Module 2's conversion lesson. Good design IS what makes a site convert.

## 📐 Layout fundamentals

> 🖼️ **DIAGRAM PLACEHOLDER:** F-pattern eye-tracking heatmap overlaid on an example homepage.

| Concept | Means | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| **Hierarchy** | Bigger/bolder = more important | One headline, one clear next priority |
| **Whitespace** | Empty space does real work | When in doubt, add more, not less |
| **F-pattern** | Eyes scan top, then left, then partway across | Important content goes top-left/top-centre |

## 🎨 Typography & colour

> 🖼️ **IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** The Daily Strength Company palette swatches — cream, linen, coral, apricot, gold, sky, sage, warm charcoal — each labelled with its use, as a worked example of restraint.

> 🔤 **Two fonts max** — one heading, one body.
> 🎨 **Small, deliberate palette** — primary, neutral background, text colour, one accent for CTAs.

**For Smith Building Co:** navy, charcoal, warm neutrals — trustworthy and established, not playful.

## 📱 Mobile-first thinking

> 🖼️ **GIF PLACEHOLDER:** Same homepage shown narrow-first, then expanding to desktop width.

> 📱 **60%+ of traffic is mobile.** Design the narrow version first, then expand — not the reverse.

1. Design at mobile width first
2. Confirm hero, phone number, CTA all work narrow
3. THEN expand to desktop

## 🛡️ Trust signals — exact placement

> 🖼️ **DIAGRAM PLACEHOLDER:** Annotated homepage wireframe with callouts pointing to each element below.

| Element | Where | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Phone number | Header, every page, `tel:` on mobile | Always available |
| Primary CTA | Above the fold, repeated | Catches every decision point |
| Real photos | High on the page | Builds trust early |
| Testimonials | Right before a CTA | Most persuasive right before action |
| ABN/certs | Footer | Supporting trust, not primary action |

## 🎭 Reading a brand: vibe → decision

> 🖼️ **IMAGE PLACEHOLDER:** Selected logo directions from the Daily Strength brand board, showing one brief producing multiple distinct, tagged directions.

**The translation process:**
1. Take the vague word ("professional," "premium")
2. Ask: compared to what? Which direction?
3. Pick a lane — minimal vs warm vs bold
4. Translate into actual choices: colour, type, photography, density

> **For Smith Building Co:** "professional, beats competitors on credibility" → navy + charcoal, real photography, minimal decoration. Reads as established and serious.
